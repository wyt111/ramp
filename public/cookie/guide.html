Here's a step-by-step guide on how to enable third-party cookies in the most popular browsers:
Google Chrome
Open Chrome and click on the three-dot menu in the top right corner.
Go to "Settings" and scroll down to the bottom.
Click on "Advanced" to expand the advanced settings.
Under the "Privacy and security" section, click on "Site settings."
Look for the "Cookies" section and click on it.
Toggle the switch to enable "Allow sites to save and read cookie data" or select "Block third-party cookies" if it's already enabled.
Firefox

Click the Tools menu from the top toolbar.

Choose Options.

Click the Privacy tab.

Under "History" select "Use custom settings for history" from the drop-down menu beside "Firefox will."

Ensure that the checkboxes for "Accept cookies from sites" and "Accept third-party cookies" are both checked.

Click OK.

For more information on Mozilla, please see Firefox Help for more instructions on enabling cookies.

Safari
Open Safari and click on "Safari" in the top menu bar.
Select "Preferences" and go to the "Privacy" tab.
Under the "Cookies and website data" section, select "Always allow" or "Allow from websites I visit" to enable third-party cookies.
Microsoft Edge
Open Microsoft Edge and click on the three-dot menu in the top right corner.
Go to "Settings" and click on "Cookies and site permissions" in the left-hand menu.
Under the "Cookies and site permissions" section, click on "Cookies and site data."
Toggle the switch next to "Block third-party cookies" to disable it.
Please note that browser versions and interfaces may vary, so the exact steps might differ slightly.
